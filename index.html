A working note from the floor

A read on where AI actually fits in the Syotos stack. And where it does not.

Syotos has picked a narrow, honest lane. Rideshare drivers who are veterans, sworn officers, or first responders. A PROTECT flow that stays active after drop-off. Sister entities across security, education, and health that all share one brand promise.

That promise lives or dies on a single operational question. How fast can Syotos vet a new driver, in a new state, without letting standards slip.

01 · The frame

Most teams put AI where traditional code belongs. They wire an LLM into problems that a rules engine and a Postgres table would finish faster, cheaper, and with better guarantees.

The useful split is closer to 60 percent traditional code and database work, 30 percent rule-based logic, 10 percent genuine AI.

60%Traditional code and database
30%Rule-based logic
10%Genuine AI

For Syotos, the onboarding funnel is mostly traditional code. Document ingestion, state-by-state compliance rules, issuing credentials. A thin and valuable AI layer sits on top. Parsing DD-214s and state certifications, surfacing gaps in applicant packets, drafting missing evidence requests.

Getting the split right is the whole game.
